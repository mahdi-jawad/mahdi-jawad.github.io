Minimized drying time and quality degradation of mango pulp during ultrasound and osmotic pre-treated hot air drying.

Research Assistantship

Abstract: Mango drying was the combination of numerous osmotic pre-treatments with ultrasound to provide adequate value when minimizing various detrimental consequences. Fresh mango slices were pre-treated by immersing them in three osmotic solutions (10% sucrose, 10% fructose, and 10% trehalose) for varying durations of 10, 20, and 30 min. Using a hot air convection dryer, the drying was done at 50, 60, and 70 °C while keeping a constant relative humidity of 30% (RH) and optimized based on the drying period, quality with textural parameters, and microbial load of dried mango slices. The optimum drying temperature was 60 °C using the trehalose-soaked samples with 20 min of ultrasound treatment was the best combination of pretreatment which preserved the highest physicochemical quality and better texture of mango slices in a shorter drying time. The reconstituted dried mangoes inferred at this state led to better textual integrity as shown by firmness (5938.33 ± 12.01), cohesiveness (13191.47 ± 42.85), elasticity (-0.17 ± 0.06), brittleness (79.63 ± 2.27), and gumminess (6.33 ± 0.6). It also kept the highest physicochemical properties, nutritional quality and showed lower color changes and lower microbial load. However, the antioxidant activity and amount of ascorbic acid were slightly decreased with increasing temperature, but the pretreatment processes sustained the amount of ascorbic acid and antioxidant activity to a great extent. According to this study, higher drying temperatures not only shorten drying times but also degrade nutritional value and physicochemical qualities. These findings will help future researchers develop unique preservation techniques by enhancing the immersion pretreatment of drying temperatures to increase the shelf life of perishable commodities like fruits and vegetables.
