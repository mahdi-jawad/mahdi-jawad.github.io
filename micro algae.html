Encapsulation efficiency with respect to physicochemical properties, storage stability and releasing behavior of polyunsaturated fatty acids extracted from microalgae.

Abstract: By employing innovative osmotic pre-treatment techniques at constant 65 °C temperature, a novel approach was developed to mitigate the alterations that typically occur during the drying of tomato slices. To initiate the pre-treatment, fresh tomato slices were immersed in five different osmotic solutions (0.2% KMS, 0.2% KMS+1% CaCl2, 65°Brix, 1% CaCl2 and 1% CaCl2 + 55% Sucrose) for a duration of 25 minutes. Subsequently, a hot air convection dryer was utilized to complete the drying process at 65 °C with the relative humidity consistently maintained at 30%. The study assessed the influence of pre-treatment strategies on various aspects of the drying process, including drying kinetics, mass transfer rate, alterations in the color of tomato slices as well as quality characteristics, drying duration and microbial presence. Throughout the drying experiment, moisture content information was collected and converted into moisture ratio (MR). Subsequently, MR information was applied to four distinct thin layer models, namely, the Page model, Logarithmic model, Henderson and Pabi’s Model and Two term exponential model. The drying process yielded a favorable curve, aligning well with the expected outcomes from the Henderson and Pabi’s and Logarithmic models. Notably, the Logarithmic model exhibited the most accurate fit among them. A superior fit for the water loss pattern was provided by The Weibull distribution model. The ideal pre-treatment technique before drying was 0.2% Potassium Metabisulphite + 1% Calcium chloride solution Considering both the physical and biochemical characteristics. At this particular condition, the dried tomato samples demonstrated improved structural integrity, as evidenced by the preservation of color, vitamin C, B vitamins, and antioxidant activity in the samples. The most significant levels of Total Phenolic Content (547.25 mg GAE/100g), Total Flavonoid Content (39. 61 mg QE/100g), and DPPH radical scavenging activity (65.31 EC50 g/100g) were observed when samples were pretreated with 0.2% Potassium Metabisulphite + 1% Calcium chloride, 0.2% Potassium Metabisulphite and 65 % Sucrose respectively. On the other hand, the shortest drying duration of 13.25 hours was achieved by employing 1% Calcium chloride pre-treatment, taking into account the time expected to reduce the moisture content to 20% in the dried item at 30% RH. According to the findings presented, it can be inferred that utilizing 0.2% Potassium Metabisulphite + 1% Calcium chloride at 65 °C is a viable method for creating premium-quality dried tomato. This approach effectively preserves the desired physicochemical and nutritional attributes. This research holds significance in addressing the growing need to produce superior grade, nutritious dehydrated tomato products.
